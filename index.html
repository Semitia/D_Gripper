DGripper: A Flexible Vision-Based Tactile Gripper with Active Surface

Jikai Xu1, 2,  Lei Wu1, 2,  Changyi Lin3,  Huazhe Xu2, 4
1Huazhong University of Science and Technology,  2Shanghai Qi Zhi Institute,  3Carnegie Mellon University,  4Tsinghua University,

Abstract

The dexterous manipulation of objects in hand can significantly enhance the capabilities of robots, yet remains a significant challenge for them. In certain robotic tasks such as object insertion and tool utilization, precise perception of the object's pose in hand is imperative.

In this work, we present an active surface tactile gripper named DGripper, equipped with an in-hand camera, capable of manipulating and exploring objects in hand and performing three-dimensional reconstruction. Overall, DGripper's end-effector surface utilizes vision-based tactile sensors to provide high-resolution tactile images, while enabling the tactile surface to actively move like a conveyor belt, possessing a total of five degrees of freedom. This greatly enhances the flexibility of the gripper, maintaining a stable structure and offering the convenience of modular use. Furthermore, it is cost-effective and easy to manufacture, requiring minimal assembly skills. Our experiments demonstrate that, leveraging tactile perception and the gripper's active movable surface, we can robustly grasp and in-hand manipulate small objects. Moreover, DGripper can grasp slender tools, perceive their poses in hand, and adjust them to a convenient hand pose for usage. Simultaneously, by altering the object's pose, the handheld camera can reconstruct the object in three dimensions, providing guidance in the subsequent task-driven control process.
